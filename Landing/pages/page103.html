Nature's fury is of different types. It can be earthquake, a volcanic eruption, cyclones and the deadliest tsunami and floods

Earthquakes are signalled by shaking of the earth. This movement is caused when earth plates colloid and stram energy is released from the Earth's crust suddenly Researchers say that every year the earth sees around 15 to 18 major earthquakes and one great earthquake and this average has been more or less the same since the year 1990, Earthquakes also invite floods, volcanic eruptions, landslides and even tsunami.

A volcanic eruption is signalled by a rupture or crack in the Earth's crust that causes the molten lava, gases, ash to reach the surface from the magma chamber. A volcano can be active, dormant or extinct depending upon its activity A volcanic eruption is followed by hazardous acid ram which causes a lot of damage to health, property and largely to agriculture.

More than 40 million hectares is prone to floods. The listing leaves out less severe disasters. like Kashmir floods in 2014, Bihar in 2007 and the annual deluge in Assam
